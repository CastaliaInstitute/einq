We start with the Xteink X4 and CrossPoint Reader, an open-source firmware stack you can flash, extend, and restore.

Platform

Calm by design

E-paper is slow, reflective, and kind to batteries. Einq treats that as a feature: surfaces update when meaning changes — morning vs evening, weekday vs weekend — not on every notification.

Time-aware

Apps schedule partial or full refreshes by clock, calendar, or cron-like rules. The screen is a function of day and time.

Open stack

CrossPoint on ESP32-C3: EPUB reading today, WiFi upload, OTA — and a path toward custom Einq sketches alongside reader mode.

Castalia apps

First-party apps in the einq repo — shared assets, build notes, and examples you can fork for your own household.

Hardware

Xteink X4

Our first supported device: a compact 4.3″ e-paper reader. Stock firmware is fine for basic files; we recommend CrossPoint for EPUB, customization, and development.

Flash CrossPoint

  • Web flasher: xteink.dve.al (Chromium)
  • CLI: esptool per upstream docs
  • Official firmware can be restored

Why open firmware

Community-driven, MIT-licensed, PlatformIO-based. You own the toolchain and can ship screen logic that runs on a schedule — the core Einq use case.

What we are building

Clock & day face

Large time, weekday, and optional secondary line (quote, transit, ritual). Full refresh at day boundaries; partial refresh on the hour.

Household rhythm

Integrations with Castalia calendars and contemplative tools — ambient awareness without another glowing rectangle.
